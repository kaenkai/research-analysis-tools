# Hopping electron transport models

## Variable Range Hopping (VRH)

The electron transport within the Mott regime of the VRH, explicitly depends on the dimensionality of the samples and has the form

$$
\sigma(T) =
\sigma_{0,M}
\exp\left[-(T_{0,M}/T)^{1/(D+1)}\right],
$$

where $\sigma_{0,M}$ is a pre-exponential factor, and $T_{0,M}$ is the characteristic temperature expressed by the formula

$$
T_{0,M} =
\frac{\beta_M}{N_F(D)k_B\xi^D},
$$

where the numerical factor $\beta_M=18.1 $  for $D=3$, and in generality, it also depends on the system's dimensionality. In low temperatures often Efros-Schklovskii regime is considered which takes into account electron-electron interactions

$$
\sigma(T) =
\sigma_{0,ES}
\exp\left[-(T_{0,ES}/T)^{1/2}\right],
$$

where the characteristic temperatures, $T_{0,ES}$, is given by the formula 

$$
T_{0,ES} =
\beta_{ES}\frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r\xi},
$$

where $\beta_{ES}=2.8$ is a numerical factor.
